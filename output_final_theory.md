**Core Category (Selective Code)**  
**Negotiated Reflexivity – the ongoing balancing act between experiential, critical‑practice learning and the structural, temporal, and epistemic constraints of the institutional context.**  

**Grounded‑theory explanation**  
Across the 222 excerpts, participants and instructors repeatedly describe a dynamic in which the desire to cultivate a deeply reflexive, hands‑on, and socially‑critical understanding of qualitative research collides with the realities of limited class time, heterogeneous student backgrounds, technology gaps, and program‑mandated curricula. When instructors foreground experiential activities (in‑class data collection, group coding, visual‑media analysis, and reflexive writing) and embed critical‑pedagogical stances (refusal, decolonial, anti‑racist framing), students experience heightened engagement, empathy, and a sense of mastery. Yet the same conditions also generate uncertainty, feelings of overload, and a perception of “unfinished” analysis because the institutional scaffolding (tight semester schedules, vague rubrics, mandatory assessment structures, and resource shortages) forces compression, superficial coverage, or ambiguous expectations. Consequently, learners oscillate between empowerment (confidence, belonging, and expanded methodological imagination) and ambivalence (frustration, anxiety, and perceived lack of guidance). The core phenomenon, therefore, is a **negotiated reflexivity**: students and teachers continuously negotiate what counts as “good” qualitative work, how much autonomy versus structure is permissible, and how to sustain critical, affective learning within the bounded affordances of the course environment. This selective code captures the central tension that drives all other axial codes—design, prior knowledge, coding practices, affect, assessment, technology, group dynamics, institutional constraints, and identity work—showing that the quality of qualitative‑methods education is produced through an ongoing, negotiated balance between experiential depth and institutional limitation.
